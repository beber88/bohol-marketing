The Israel-Philippines Tax Treaty: What Every Israeli Abroad Should Know

Whether you live in New York, London, Berlin, or Melbourne, if you hold Israeli citizenship, there's a tax treaty between Israel and the Philippines (signed 1992, effective January 1997) that works in your favor.

Here's what it covers:

Capital gains tax:
- Philippines: 6% on property sales
- Israel: 25% on foreign property gains
- Treaty prevents double taxation, you pay in PH, credit against Israeli obligation

Rental income:
- Taxed in the Philippines at source
- Tax credit available in Israel (or your country of residence)
- Net effective rate significantly lower than most Western jurisdictions

Why this matters for property investment:

A villa in Panglao, Bohol, Philippines:
- Price: USD 528,000 (PHP 32,500,000)
- 263.78 sqm, 4 bedrooms, private pool
- Projected monthly income: PHP 395,000 (~USD 6,400) with professional management
- Projected gross yield: 14-20%, projected net yield: 7-9%

Compare that projected after-tax yield to:
- US rental property after federal + state tax: 4-7% net
- Israeli rental after tax + arnona: 2-3% net
- UK rental after income tax + stamp duty: 3-5% net

Three ownership structures available:
1. Deed of Assignment, simplest, most popular
2. 99-year Leasehold (RA 12252, September 2025)
3. Domestic Corporation, suitable for multi-property holdings

Each structure has different treaty implications. The right one depends on where you're tax resident.
