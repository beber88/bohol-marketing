Two Resort Islands. Very Different Numbers.

Korean investors know resort island real estate. Jeju has been the domestic benchmark for decades. But how does it compare to Panglao, Bohol, Philippines?

Jeju Island, South Korea:
- Median villa price: ~KRW 400M
- Net yield: 3.5-5.5%
- Seasonal (peak: July-August, slow: November-February)
- Oversupply concerns in key resort areas
- High property tax and maintenance costs
- Domestic tourism dependent

Panglao Island, Bohol, Philippines:
- Luxury villa price (4BR, 263.78 sqm, private pool): KRW ~740M-797M (~USD 528,000-569,000)
- Projected gross yield: 14-20%, projected net yield: 7-9%
- Year-round tropical climate, consistent Airbnb demand
- Premium villas achieve 65%+ occupancy
- Projected income: PHP 395,000/month (professional management)
- International tourism base (Korean #1 source, Japanese, Chinese, European, American visitors)
- 1.4 million tourists in 2025, growing 4.18% year over year

The comparison:
- Yield: Panglao projected net 7-9% vs Jeju 3.5-5.5%
- Seasonality: Panglao is year-round; Jeju is heavily seasonal
- Growth trajectory: Bohol-Panglao International Airport (2.22M passengers), Japan charters launching May 2026

Both islands offer beach lifestyle, natural beauty, and resort-quality living. The difference is the return profile and the year-round demand.
