Airbnb Yields Across Southeast Asia: A Data-Driven Comparison

If you are evaluating short-term rental investments in Southeast Asia, yield is the metric that matters. Not brochure promises, but sourced data and professional projections.

Here is the yield data across the region's top resort destinations:

Bali, Indonesia:
- Typical 4BR villa: USD 800K-1.5M
- Average occupancy: ~63% (source: STR/AirDNA regional data)
- Net yield: 3-4%
- Risk: regulatory uncertainty on short-term rentals, overtourism backlash

Phuket, Thailand:
- Typical 4BR villa: USD 900K-2M (~USD 2,000/sqm)
- Average occupancy: 50-60%
- Net yield: 3-6% (source: Phuket property market reports)
- Risk: mature market, seasonal monsoon, high supply

Da Nang, Vietnam:
- Typical 4BR villa: USD 400K-700K
- Average occupancy: 40-55%
- Net yield: 4-7%
- Risk: foreign ownership restrictions, currency controls

Panglao, Bohol, Philippines:
- 4BR villa (263.78 sqm, private pool, 60 sec to beach): USD 528,000-569,000
- Market average: $68/night, 29% occupancy
- Premium managed villas: PHP 14,000-22,000/night, 65%+ occupancy
- Projected income: PHP 395,000/month (~USD 6,400) with professional management
- Projected gross yield: 14-20%, projected net yield: 7-9%
- Context: 1.4M tourists in 2025, airport handled 2.22M passengers

The gap between market average and premium managed properties is where the opportunity sits. Professional management, premium positioning, and consistent quality push occupancy from 29% to 65%+ and nightly rates from $68 to PHP 14,000-22,000.
