Seoul Apartment vs Bohol Villa: A Yield Comparison

Seoul apartment (Gangnam, 84 sqm, 2BR):
- Purchase price: ~KRW 1.2B (~USD 870,000)
- Net yield: 2.3-3.0%
- Tenant turnover, maintenance, jeonse risk
- Capital appreciation slowing after 2024 peak

Panglao villa (263.78 sqm, 4BR, private pool, 60 sec to beach), Panglao, Bohol, Philippines:
- Purchase price: KRW ~740M (~USD 528,000 / PHP 32,500,000)
- Projected income: PHP 395,000/month (~USD 6,400 / ~KRW 9M)
- Projected gross yield: 14-20%, projected net yield: 7-9%
- Professional property management included
- Premium villas achieve 65%+ occupancy at PHP 14,000-22,000/night

The math:
- Seoul apartment at KRW 1.2B generating 2.3-3.0% net = KRW 28-36M/year
- Panglao villa at KRW ~740M with projected 7-9% net = KRW 52-67M/year

That is roughly 2x the net yield, at 38% lower entry cost.
