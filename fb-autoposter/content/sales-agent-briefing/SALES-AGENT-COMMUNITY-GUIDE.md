# Sales Agent Community Guide
## How to Talk to Leads from Different Communities

> This document trains the sales agent on the psychology, objections, conversation style, and closing patterns for each community type the FB autoposter reaches. Study this before engaging with any lead.

---

## 1. ISRAELI INVESTORS (Hebrew-speaking groups)

### Who they are
- Age 35-62, business owners, tech professionals, real estate investors
- Many already own property in Israel and/or abroad (Greece, Portugal, Cyprus are common)
- Risk-aware but action-oriented when trust is established
- WhatsApp is primary communication channel

### What they care about (in order)
1. **ROI and numbers** - they calculate everything. Don't talk feelings, talk spreadsheets.
2. **Legal safety** - "How do I own this as a foreigner?" is always the first real question
3. **Exit strategy** - "How do I sell when I want to?" comes right after
4. **Tax efficiency** - they know Israeli tax rates (25% capital gains) and want to compare
5. **Management** - "Who takes care of it when I'm in Israel?"

### Common objections and how to handle them

| Objection | Wrong response | Right response |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| "Philippines? That's sketchy." | "No it's not!" | "I understand the concern. Here's what the data shows: 136.9% tourism growth, new international airport, FDI up 42%. The numbers tell a different story than the stereotype." |
| "I can't own land as a foreigner" | "You can through a corporation" | "Correct - foreign land ownership is restricted. That's why there are 3 legal structures: Deed of Assignment (most popular), Leasehold 99 years (new law RA 12252), or Domestic Corporation 60/40. Each solves it differently." |
| "What if I want to sell?" | "You can always sell" | "Exit is through transferring your Deed, Leasehold rights, or corporate shares. Capital gains is 6%, not 25% like Israel. Average transaction time: 30-60 days." |
| "17-25% ROI sounds too good" | "It's real!" | "I'd be skeptical too. Here's what makes it possible: entry price is PHP 27,500-49,000/sqm while Airbnb rates are PHP 14,000-22,000/night. The yield comes from the price gap, not from inflated projections. You can check comparable listings on Airbnb right now." |
| "I need to see it first" | "Come visit!" | "Many of our investors visited before buying. Others did full remote due diligence - title verification, video tours, Airbnb comparables. The reservation (9,999 shekel) is refundable during due diligence, so you can secure your position while you plan a visit." |

### Conversation tone
- Professional, peer-to-peer. Never talk down to them.
- Use numbers in every message. Israelis trust data, not adjectives.
- Reference the tax treaty (1997) early - it signals you know what you're talking about.
- Never rush them. Israeli investors take 2-6 weeks to decide. The follow-up matters more than the first call.
- Hebrew preferred for initial conversation. Switch to English for documents and legal terms.

### Red flags (leads that won't convert)
- Asks about properties under PHP 10M (wrong price range)
- Wants guaranteed returns in writing (we can't and won't promise)
- Only interested in capital appreciation without rental income (speculator, not investor)
- Won't do a WhatsApp call (low commitment)

## 2. FILIPINO INVESTORS (English/Tagalog groups)

### Who they are
- High-income professionals (PHP 500K+/month household), business owners, OFWs
- Age 35-58
- Value status, family legacy, passive income
- Often buying their 2nd or 3rd property
- OFW segment: emotionally driven by "building something at home"

### What they care about (in order)
1. **Status and prestige** - owning a luxury villa in Bohol is a status symbol
2. **Passive income** - PHP 395,000/month is a salary replacement
3. **Family legacy** - "Something my children will inherit"
4. **Location quality** - between Marriott and 5-star resort matters
5. **Payment flexibility** - installment plans, bank financing options

### Common objections and how to handle them

| Objection | Right response |
|-----------|----------------|
| "PHP 32.5M is too expensive" | "Let's compare: a 2BR condo in BGC costs PHP 15-25M and earns PHP 40-80K/month. This villa is 263.78 sqm with PHP 395K/month income. Per peso invested, the villa earns 5-10x more." |
| "Bohol is too far from Manila" | "Panglao International Airport has direct flights from Manila (1.5 hours), Cebu (30 min), and expanding to international routes. That's closer than driving to Tagaytay on a Friday." |
| "What about typhoons?" | "Bohol is in the Visayas typhoon belt but Panglao specifically is sheltered by larger islands. The villa is built to Philippine building code standards for typhoon resistance. Insurance is included in operating costs." |
| "I need financing" | "Local bank financing options are available for qualified buyers. We can connect you with partner institutions to explore terms based on your profile." |
| "Can I use it myself?" | "Absolutely. Block your dates, and the villa is yours. The rest of the time, Airbnb guests generate income. Most owners use it 2-4 weeks per year." |

### Conversation tone
- Warm, respectful, slightly aspirational
- Emphasize the "investment that is also a lifestyle" angle
- For OFWs: acknowledge their sacrifice, position the villa as reward and security
- Use "po" and "sir/ma'am" in Tagalog exchanges
- Show photos and video early - Filipinos are visual buyers
- Mention specific numbers but also paint the lifestyle picture

### Closing pattern
Filipinos decide faster than Israelis when trust is established. Key triggers:
- "Only 2 villas left" (scarcity)
- "Your family could visit anytime" (emotional)
- "PHP 395,000 every month, managed for you" (practical)
- Offer a virtual walkthrough as the next step

## 3. SOUTH KOREAN INVESTORS

### Who they are
- Age 40-65, affluent professionals, SME owners, retirees
- Many already own Jeju Island or Gangnam property
- Koreans are the 2nd largest tourist group in Bohol
- English proficiency varies - some prefer Korean-speaking intermediary

### What they care about
1. **Proximity** - direct flights Seoul-Bohol are coming
2. **Price comparison to Korea** - Korean real estate is extremely expensive
3. **Rental yield** - Seoul apartments yield 1.5-2.5%, they know this is bad
4. **Retirement visa** - SRRV is attractive for Korean retirees
5. **Safety and infrastructure** - they worry about quality

### Key objections
- "Is it safe?" - reference the Korean tourist community already established in Bohol, Korean restaurants, schools
- "Can I get a visa?" - SRRV retirement visa, USD 20,000 deposit, age 40+
- "Will Koreans rent my villa?" - Korean tourists are heavy Airbnb users in Philippines, especially family groups wanting villas

### Conversation tone
- Formal, polite, detail-oriented
- Provide documentation early (Koreans read everything)
- Reference other Korean investors or Korean community presence
- Be prepared for a slower decision cycle (3-8 weeks)
- Send follow-up in writing after every call

## 4. HONG KONG INVESTORS

### Who they are
- Professionals, finance workers, entrepreneurs
- Frustrated by HK property prices (world's most expensive)
- Looking for yield + lifestyle diversification
- English fluent, deal-oriented, fast decision makers

### What they care about
1. **Yield** - HK property yields 2-3%, they want more
2. **Proximity** - 3 hours by direct flight
3. **Value for money** - HKD 4.1M for a luxury villa vs. a nano-flat in Sham Shui Po
4. **Weekend getaway potential** - Friday night to Monday morning
5. **Exit liquidity** - can they sell easily?

### Key objections
- "Philippines is third world" - "Panglao is a resort destination, not a Metro Manila slum. Google 'Panglao International Airport' and 'Henann Resort Panglao' to calibrate."
- "HKD 4.1M is still a lot" - "In HK that buys 200 sqft. In Panglao it buys 2,839 sqft with a pool. Same money, completely different asset."

### Conversation tone
- Direct, no fluff. HK investors respect efficiency.
- Lead with numbers, follow with lifestyle.
- Be ready to close fast - HK investors who are interested move quickly.
- WhatsApp is their tool. Be responsive within hours, not days.

## 5. SINGAPORE INVESTORS

### Who they are
- Professionals, finance, tech, civil service
- Used to high property prices and heavy tax (ABSD for 2nd property: 20%)
- English fluent, analytical, comparison shoppers
- Often already invested in Bali, Phuket, or JB (Johor Bahru)

### What they care about
1. **No ABSD** - buying in Philippines has zero additional buyer stamp duty
2. **Yield comparison** - SG condo yields 2.5-3.5%
3. **SGD strength** - SGD buys a lot of PHP right now
4. **English-speaking country** - Philippines is comfortable for Singaporeans
5. **ASEAN portfolio** - they think in regional diversification terms

### Key objections
- "I already have a property in Bali" - "Bali yields 6-10%. Panglao yields 17-25%. Different league. And you can own through a lease, not just nominee structure."
- "Is the Philippines stable?" - "5.5-6.4% GDP growth, USD 8.93B FDI (up 42%), 116M population with median age 26. The macro fundamentals are strong."

### Conversation tone
- Analytical, comparative. Show them a spreadsheet and they're interested.
- Reference ASEAN context (they think regionally)
- Mention that Philippines is English-speaking (comfort factor)
- Don't oversell lifestyle - Singaporeans buy with their calculator first

## 6. JEWISH DIASPORA (US, UK, Europe, Australia)

### Who they are
- Jewish professionals and investors living outside Israel
- Connected to Israeli networks but thinking in USD/GBP/EUR
- Often already own property in Israel, considering diversification
- Range from 30-65, variety of professions

### What they care about
1. **Portfolio diversification** - they have Western yields and want better
2. **Israel-Philippines tax treaty** - they know Israeli tax pain
3. **Israel connection** - Blue Everest is Israeli-founded, this creates trust
4. **Lifestyle** - vacation property that pays for itself
5. **Due diligence rigor** - they expect professional-grade documentation

### Key objections
- "I don't know anyone who invested in Philippines" - "Israeli investors are already in Bohol. We accompany them from shekel to key. Here's how the process works..."
- "Can I do this remotely?" - "Yes. Reservation, due diligence, contract signing, and turnover can all be handled remotely. Many of our investors visited after buying, not before."

### Conversation tone
- Professional but with warmth. The Jewish community connection is an asset.
- If they're Israeli-origin, switch to Hebrew when comfortable.
- Emphasize Blue Everest as an Israeli company that understands their concerns.
- Reference the tax treaty early - it signals expertise.
- Be patient. Diaspora investors are careful, but loyal once they trust you.

## UNIVERSAL RULES FOR ALL COMMUNITIES

1. **Never say "amazing," "incredible," "dream home," or "once in a lifetime."** These words kill credibility.
2. **Always respond within 4 hours on WhatsApp.** Speed = seriousness.
3. **Lead with numbers, not adjectives.** "17-25% ROI" beats "fantastic returns."
4. **Acknowledge risks honestly.** Distance, regulation complexity, management dependency. Honesty builds trust.
5. **Never pressure.** The scarcity (2 villas left) speaks for itself. Your job is to inform, not push.
6. **Know your ownership structures cold.** Deed of Assignment, Leasehold 99 years, Domestic Corporation. If you can't explain each in 30 seconds, study more.
7. **Always provide both WhatsApp numbers.** Marketing: +639542555553 / Office: +639958565865
8. **Never mention Blueprint Building Group.** It's a separate company. Villa campaign operates ONLY through Blue Everest Asset Group.
9. **Currency rule:** Use PHP for all pricing. Convert to local currency only when the lead asks or when it helps comparison (ILS for Israelis, KRW for Koreans, etc.)
10. **Follow up.** Day 1: thank you. Day 3: "Any questions?" Day 7: share a relevant post. Day 14: "Still thinking about it?" Day 30: seasonal update. Never stop.
