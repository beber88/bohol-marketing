## AIRPORT COPY

### English

**A Gateway That Is Already Above Its Original Capacity**

Bohol-Panglao International Airport was originally designed to handle around 2 million passengers per year. In 2025, the airport already welcomed approximately 2.22 million passengers, making it one of the busiest gateways in the Philippines.

**Long-term expansion under a world-class operator**

Aboitiz InfraCapital has taken over the airport under a 30-year public-private partnership, with a committed multi-billion-peso modernization and expansion program. According to Aboitiz and Philippine government reports, the ongoing upgrades aim to increase the airport's handling capacity from about 2.0 million passengers today to roughly 2.5 million passengers in the short term, and to nearly 4 million passengers annually by 2030.

For investors, this means Panglao is backed by a long-term infrastructure plan that is explicitly designed to accommodate sustained growth in tourist arrivals and international flights.

Sources: Wikipedia (BPIA), Bohol Gov, SunStar, Aboitiz Eyes, Travel Daily Media, Asian Journal, Daily Tribune, Inquirer Business

## TOURISM COPY

### English

**Tourism at Record Levels - and Still Growing**

Bohol's visitor arrivals have rebounded strongly from the pandemic, climbing from 535,803 visitors in 2022 to 1,369,945 in 2024. In 2025, the province welcomed about 1.4 million tourists, representing a year-on-year increase of roughly 4.18 percent, driven by a surge in foreign visitors.

Panglao Island is at the heart of this growth. It has been named one of Skyscanner's global "Top 10 Trending Destinations for 2025" - the only Philippine destination on the list - after recording a 77 percent jump in flight searches in early 2024. This combination of record arrivals, a trending global profile, and ongoing airport expansion positions Panglao as one of Southeast Asia's most compelling emerging resort markets.

Sources: PPDO Bohol, Bohol Island News, Inquirer, PIA Central Visayas, Skyscanner, GMA Network, Bilyonaryo
